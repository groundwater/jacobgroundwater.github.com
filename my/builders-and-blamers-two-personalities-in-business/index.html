Builders and Blamers: Two Personalities in Business

Posted May 20, 2012 by Jacob Groundwater & filed under Journals.

I have experienced two general types of people in my career. The first type are builders, they will take an idea and try to build off of it. They take concepts and play with them, and experiment with them in their heads. They tend to think of precice details as just that, details.

The other type is a highly critical group whose first response is to state all the reasons why an idea is flawed. They treat your initial explaination akin to a business proposal, it must be perfectly developed. If there are unclear areas they consider the entire idea a failure.

The latter type finds their way into business all too often. They are the pecimists of the group, always claiming they know best because they constantly bet against their own team. Should you succeed, you’ll be lucky to get an acknowledgement, much more likely they will point out how unrealistic expectations were not met. Should you fail, they will tap themselves on the back and ask for a promotion.

The latter type is poison to creative people. I’m sure they have a use somewhere, but nowhere that requries risk. They are not better at guarding against risk because their default answer is “try nothing”. All risk is equally averse to them.

Builders on the other hand will explore a risk as much as possible before comimtting or abandoning an idea. A builder is the type to rapidly iterate many small ideas in an attempt to find something that works. Builders believe that details can only be solved when realized, not in a meeting somewhere.

Blamers attempt to shed responsibility, since they ultimately believe everything new will fail. Builders take on responsibility.

Avoid blamers. Find Builders.
